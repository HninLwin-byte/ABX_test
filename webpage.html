Data Augmentation of Alaryngeal Speech Using Voice Conversion with Timbre Enhanced Loss

Abstract

The primary goal of voice conversion is to transform the vocal characteristics of speech while preserving its linguistic content. In this paper, we introduce a data augmentation technique for alaryngeal speech using voice conversion within the MaskCycleGAN-VC framework.

Baseline Models

  • CycleGAN-VC2 - Voice Conversion using CycleGAN
  • CycleGAN-VC3 - Improved CycleGAN-based Voice Conversion
  • MaskCycleGAN-VC - Voice Conversion with Masked CycleGAN

Training Settings

As part of the preprocessing stage, Mel-spectrograms underwent normalization using training dataset statistics. Employing a least-squares GAN as our GAN objective, the training process spanned 5k iterations, with an Adam optimizer employed. Specifically, the converter and discriminator utilized learning rates of 0.0002 and 0.0001, respectively. Momentum terms were established as β1 = 0.5 and β2 = 0.999. The batch size was defined as 1, where individual training instances encompassed 64 randomly cropped frames, approximately equating to a duration of 0.75 seconds. Hyperparameters, λcyc, λid, and λresem were configured at values of 10, 5, and 0.0001.
